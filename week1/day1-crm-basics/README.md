# CRM Basics

## Introduction

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) is a system used by companies to manage customer information, communication, sales, and services in one place. CRM helps businesses organize customer data and improve relationships with customers. Instead of storing information in spreadsheets or notebooks, companies use CRM software to track everything digitally and efficiently.

Salesforce is one of the most popular CRM platforms used by companies around the world. It is a cloud-based platform, which means users can access it from anywhere using the internet. Salesforce helps businesses automate tasks, manage customer data, track sales, and improve productivity.

---

# Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it helps them manage business processes more easily. It stores customer information securely and allows teams to work together efficiently. Salesforce also provides automation tools, reports, dashboards, and customization features that help companies save time and increase productivity.

Some important reasons companies use Salesforce are:

- Centralized customer data management
- Automation of repetitive tasks
- Better communication between teams
- Easy report and dashboard creation
- Cloud-based access from anywhere
- Support for low-code and no-code development

---

# Salesforce Objects

Salesforce stores information using objects. Objects are similar to tables in a database.

## Account

An Account represents a company, organization, or business entity.

Examples:
- College
- Hospital
- Company
- Customer organization

Accounts store information related to organizations such as name, address, phone number, and business details.

---

## Contact

A Contact represents a person associated with an account.

Examples:
- Student
- Patient
- Employee
- Customer

Contacts store personal details like name, email, phone number, and address.

---

## Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a possible business deal or future conversion.

Examples:
- Student admission process
- Product sale
- Service agreement
- Event registration

Opportunities help companies track potential sales or business growth.

---

# Business Flow in CRM

In Salesforce, business processes usually follow this flow:

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

A lead is a person who shows interest in a service or product. After verifying the lead information, the lead becomes a contact. When there is a chance of successful business or conversion, it becomes an opportunity. After the deal is completed successfully, the person becomes a customer.

---

# Real World Mapping — College Admission System

| Salesforce Concept | Real World Example |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Account | College |
| Contact | Student |
| Lead | Student inquiry |
| Opportunity | Admission process |

In a college admission system, students first show interest by making inquiries. These inquiries are considered leads. After collecting student details, they become contacts. When the admission process starts, it becomes an opportunity. Once admission is confirmed, the student becomes part of the institution.
